PROGRAMA PARA VALIDACION DE LO MODELO MATEMATICO DE UN SISTEMA UVGI
Mathematical modeling of the UV disinfection process provides a basis for sizing ultraviolet disinfection equipment and for interpreting test results.
UV Lamp Modeling
The fraction of radiative irradiance that leaves the cylindrical body and arrives ata differential area (Modest 1993)
Aplicacion de las ecuaciones a las lamparas philips.
clc,clear all,close all;
r=13*(1/10);
l=167*(1/10);
x=5:1:105;
L=l/r;
for k=1:length(x)
H(k)=x(k)/r;
X(k)=(1+H(k))^2+L^2;
Y(k)=(1-H(k))^2+L^2;
M(k)=sqrt((H(k)-1)/(H(k)+1));
F(k)=(L/(pi*H(k)))*(((1/L)*atan(L/(sqrt(H(k)^2-1))))-(atan(M(k)))+((X(k)-2*H(k))/(sqrt(X(k)*Y(k))))*atan(M(k)*sqrt(X(k)*Y(k))));
end
Ir=(2.3*1e6)/(2*pi*r*l)*F;
figure(4)
plot(x,Ir,"k--")
grid on
legend('I')
title('Single Stage Decay')
xlabel('Distance [cm]'); ylabel('[Irradiance uW/cm^2]');
Ir=(Ir*100^2)/(1E6);
UV Disinfection Modeling
The ultraviolet disinfection processmay involve simple exponential decay, or a more complex function composed of two or more decay processes
UV Exposure Dose
Microbes exposed to UV irradiation are subject to an exposure dose (fluence) that is a function of the irradiance multiplied by the exposure time.
D=UV exposure dose
=exposure time s
=Irradiance, , parametro mas importante a calcular depende el sistema
Et=0:0.1:30;
D=Et*Ir(101)*0.5;
D1=Et*Ir(50)*0.5;
figure(1)
plot(Et,D,"k--",Et,D1,"r--");
grid on
legend('D1','D2');
title('Exposure Dose ')
xlabel(' Et[s]'); ylabel('[D J/M^2]');
Single Stage Decay
UVGI design purposes provided the UV dose is within first order parameters. This is because disinfection rates of 9099% can generally be achieved in the first stage of decay, and this is adequate for most design purposes.
